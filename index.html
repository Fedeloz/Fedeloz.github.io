Federico Lozano‑Cuadra

Ph.D. Researcher in AI for Space Communications (Fulbright)

Federico works on machine learning for space communications, with a focus on Low Earth Orbit satellite constellations, autonomous lunar rover swarms, and decentralized intelligent networks. His work spans multi-agent reinforcement learning, foundational models, and communication-aware AI for autonomous space systems.

Research

Learning-based lunar rover exploration

This work develops communication-aware AI for autonomous lunar rover swarms, combining foundation models with multi-agent reinforcement learning and graph attention to support decentralized routing and decision making in delay-tolerant networks. It is carried out with NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory and Johns Hopkins University ACE Lab.

Multi-agent deep reinforcement learning for distributed satellite routing

This line of work studies decentralized routing in LEO satellite constellations using multi-agent deep reinforcement learning, including continual learning under changing network conditions. An open-source simulator supports training, evaluation, and comparison of routing policies in dynamic satellite topologies.

SatCom-TELMA/MA-DRL_Routing_Simulator — · —

Multicast traffic optimization in mesh satellite constellations

This work addresses joint routing and downlink resource allocation for multicast traffic in mesh non-terrestrial networks, aiming to improve delivery efficiency across interconnected LEO constellations. The focus is on scalable optimization methods for shared traffic demands in dynamic satellite mesh architectures.
